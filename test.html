Dr. Twumasi

Keynote Speaker

Dr Afua Animwaa Asante Twumasi, 50 years old (1st February 1974). I have been married for 23 years. I have four amazing girls. I am a Public Health Physician Specialist with a speciality in family Reproductive Health. I have worked for twenty-three years as a medical doctor. I am a public health specialist with expertise in family Reproductive Health. I have headed two polyclinics, Madina Polyclinic and Adabraka Polyclinic, and worked as the Municipal Director of Health Services at Ghana Health Service for thirteen years. I am a National facilitator for Infection Prevention and Control for Ghana Health Service. I am also a Regional facilitator for Quality Improvement and Supportive Supervision for Ghana Health Service Greater Accra. I teach at the Ghana College of Physicians and Surgeons. I love God and love working with youth. I support my husband in working with the youth and young Adults in the Church. I also teach the underprivileged teen class in my church. I love evangelism and head the medical outreach team at Grace Baptist Church. I am also a bible studies teacher of the youth. This I do with my husband passionately. I am passionate about the things of God, family and Health.

Stacey Nzingha Hill

Guest Speaker

Stacy Nzinga Hill, a native of Chicago, Illinois, holds a BS in Political Science and African-American Studies from the University of Illinois at Chicago, followed by an M.Ed. in Secondary Education/Social Science from Alabama A&M University. With a background spanning various teaching roles in both Chicago and Philadelphia charter schools over 20 years, Stacy has been deeply engaged in educational initiatives and organizations. As a co-founder of the Chicago Grassroots Curriculum Taskforce and the Philadelphia Black History Collaborative, she has contributed significantly to curriculum development and teacher training. Stacy's extensive international experience includes teaching and studying in countries such as Senegal, The Gambia, Ghana, Tanzania, and Spain. Currently, she serves as the Curriculum Coordinator for the American Institute of Resource and Human Development Fulbright-Hays Group Projects Abroad Program, leading curriculum development efforts. Stacy is also actively involved in rewriting the Social Studies curriculum for the School District of Philadelphia, demonstrating her commitment to educational excellence and innovation.

Nannete Atuahene

Speaker

Nannette Atuahene was born and raised in the United States, to a mother and father from Kumasi, Ghana. She splits the year, living between Ghana and California. She is a real estate investor and broker for over 20 years, and founder of On Our Watch Global, LLC, a leadership development and orgazational effectiveness consultancy. She also contributes her time and expertise to the community on the streets of Ghana, and to several Ghanaian NGOs, primarily dealing with empowering youth.

Isabel Chitende

Guest Speaker

Isabel Chitende is a dedicated mental health advocate, leveraging her platform as a radio and podcast host to amplify awareness and support. Currently pursuing her Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) degree with a major in Human Resources at Academic City University, Isabel combines her passion for people with a creative flair in digital marketing and content creation. As a skilled creative writer and public speaker, she strives to inspire and educate others on various topics close to her heart. Comfort Esonu

Panelist

Comfort Esonu, a dynamic Information Technology student in Accra, Ghana, hails from Togo, where she spent her formative years. Currently in her second year of study with a commendable GPA of 3.56/4.0, Comfort's educational journey also includes an economics background. Beyond academia, Comfort has garnered numerous accolades, including certificates in Test Dome and EF Set, as well as being a runner-up in a Spelling Bee competition and recognized for her impact as a peer tutor. With a rich work history as a Software Developer Intern and experience as a Personal Assistant, Comfort has showcased her versatility. Her skills span a wide range, from programming languages to soft skills such as logical thinking and effective communication. Engaged in extra-curricular activities, Comfort is a member of the Robotics and Dancing Clubs in Accra, Ghana, demonstrating her commitment to both technology and leadership development. Her community service initiatives, such as volunteering at schools and organizing environmental programs, reflect her dedication to making a positive impact. Comfort's interests extend beyond academia and work, including videography, photography, swimming, and cooking. In essence, Comfort Esonu is a well-rounded individual with a diverse skill set, passion for technology, and a commitment to community service
